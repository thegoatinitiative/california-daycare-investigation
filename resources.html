Tools and information for investigating childcare fraud in California

Important: This dashboard identifies patterns commonly associated with fraud but does not prove fraud occurred. Always conduct proper investigation and consult with appropriate authorities before taking action.

üìã File a Complaint

If you suspect fraud or unsafe conditions at a childcare facility, you can file a complaint with the California Department of Social Services.

Community Care Licensing Division (CCLD)

CCLD investigates complaints about licensed childcare facilities including health, safety, and licensing violations.

  • Complaints can be made anonymously
  • CCLD must investigate within 10 days
  • You can track complaint status online
File a CCLD Complaint

Report Fraud to the OIG

For suspected financial fraud involving federal childcare funds (CCDF, CACFP), report to the Office of Inspector General.

Report to HHS OIG

üìä Understanding Our Data

Data Sources

  • Facility Data: CA Community Care Licensing Division (CCLD) public database
  • PPP Loans: SBA Paycheck Protection Program loan data
  • Violations: CCLD inspection and complaint records

Risk Score Methodology

Our risk scores (1-10) are based on patterns from major fraud cases:

  • COVID-Era License (2020-2022): +2 points
  • Closed/Inactive Status: +2 points
  • Short Operation (<24 months): +3 points
  • Shared Phone Number: +2 points
  • Generic Name: +1 point

Limitations

This analysis cannot detect:

  • Facilities that never obtained a license
  • Fraud at facilities with no public red flags
  • The actual amount of fraudulent claims

High risk scores indicate patterns worth investigating‚Äîthey do not prove fraud.
